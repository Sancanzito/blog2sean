The Philosophy Behind the Computer: A Journey Toward the Future

The future doesn't always follow the path we plan — sometimes it leads us to places we never imagined, but exactly where we're meant to be.

"Life is what happens to you while you're busy making other plans." - John Lennon

From Computer Engineering to Education

My supposed dream in life was to become a computer engineer, inspired by my classmates and my desire to create my own game. However, life took a different turn, and I believe God had another plan for me. Today, I am pursuing a Bachelor of Secondary Education major in Science at CTU Moalboal. I aspire to excel in my field and, through God's grace and my dedication, to build a brighter future — not just for myself, but for others as well.

The Early Dream: Becoming a Scientist

My first dream ever was to become a scientist. During my elementary years, I was endlessly curious about how and why the world works the way it does. Every time we went to the city, my first request was to buy books about science — books about the Earth, dinosaurs, insects, and even encyclopedias. Whenever I stayed at my cousin's house during vacations, I enjoyed reading her encyclopedia, which further sparked my interest in science. That curiosity never faded, and even now, science continues to inspire me.

The Gaming Passion That Shaped My Path

My second dream was to become a computer engineer. I was drawn to my love for games, especially RPGs with their amazing storylines. My favorites were the Pokémon series on Game Boy and Yu-Gi-Oh!, a trading card game I played before discovering Mobile Legends. I was always fascinated by how games work and how they are made. Unfortunately, due to certain circumstances, I wasn't able to pursue that course — but I still carry the same curiosity and passion for learning on the path I walk today.

A New Direction: Becoming a Science Teacher

Now, I am studying to become a science teacher, a journey that began when I applied for a free scholarship. The course I originally wanted in the city was already full, so I chose to apply for BSED Science here at CTU Moalboal. Through God's favor, I was one of the few who got accepted. And so here I am, continuing my studies with hope and determination — aiming to build a stable career, live a meaningful life, and inspire future learners to follow their dreams just as I did.

"The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams." - Eleanor Roosevelt

Conclusion: Embracing God's Plan

In conclusion, there are moments in life when our plans and dreams don't go the way we expect. But God's plans are greater than ours — they shape us in ways we may not understand at first. Even though I didn't take my original dream course, I am thankful for where I am now and for the opportunities He has given me. My dreams for the future are simple yet full of hope: to earn a good living, to visit Japan, Italy, and Korea, and to continue growing as a person and as a teacher.

The future remains unwritten, but one thing is certain — I will keep walking forward with faith, passion, and gratitude, trusting that every step I take brings me closer to the life God has prepared for me.

About the Author

A future educator passionate about science, technology, and the journey of life. Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Secondary Education major in Science at CTU Moalboal, with dreams of inspiring the next generation of learners.
